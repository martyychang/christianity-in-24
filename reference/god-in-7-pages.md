# God Explained in 7 Pages: The Basics to Learn (and Unlearn)

## Faith (n.)

**Definition**: *firm belief in something for which there is no proof*

Faith in the context of God comes in two flavors: faith that God does exist and faith that God does not. There is no wrong answer, as God's existence by definition can neither be proven nor disproven.

Your choice in the matter is similar to voting in a two-party system. You can vote for God, vote against God, abstain undecided and also change your mind from time to time. While abstaining is an option, affirming one of the faiths is best so as to avoid having the choice made for you by someone else.

Given that the biological cycle of life and death in a world without God is already well understood through science, let's continue our tour through the idea of a world *with* God. The next six pages aim to clarify common misconceptions before returning to our point of departure.

## God (n.)

**Definition**: *the Being perfect in power, wisdom, and goodness who is worshipped as creator and ruler of the universe*

Merriam-Webster's definition above exposes the popular notion that God is perfect. But perfect is a subjective and unreliable opinion unlikely to be shared by two random people. Let's instead consider God to have flaws and to be *imperfect* but nonetheless the creator and ruler of our universe.

God created humanity. God experiences the same emotions we experience: joy, sadness, anger, disgust, fear, love and more. While God's powers very likely have limits, compared relatively to humans God is all-powerful and eternal. And how God wields those powers through the emotional ups and downs of raising humanity brings us to the next stop on this tour: the Bible.

## Bible (n.)

**Definition**: *a publication that is preeminent especially in authoritativeness or wide readership*

The Bible is a collection of literature written by various authors over a long period of time, collected posthumously and curated into a book by a committee of other people. Maybe the anthology is divinely inspired, and maybe it's not. But objectively as of 2021, the Bible in its myriad translations is the world's best-selling and most widely distributed book. One reason is that many people look to the Bible as a means of understanding who God is and what God wants.

Centuries ago, the few literate people in power mystified and misused the Bible to advance their own selfish ends. While this unfortunate practice lingers today, the rise of literacy gives you the chance to call bluffs, to debunk myths and to seek the truth for yourself.

In the Bible you will find a remarkably consistent storyline that features a loving, feeling creator. You will find a God who does have a track record of punishing us in bloody wrath but ultimately wants humanity to grow, learn and thrive.

## Promise (v.)

**Definition**: *to pledge to do, bring about, or provide*

The Bible describes many promises God made to various people over time, encouraging us to do what God considers to be the right thing. God’s most current and personally relevant promise is that each of us may be granted eternal life.

While many have imagined and reimagined versions of eternal life, no one knows for sure whether and how it works. Regardless, people who believe generally agree on two things: Eternal life is significantly longer than the 78.9 years expected by the average American, and eternal life comes sometime after death.

A third point believers usually accept is that God grants eternal life conditionally, although the exact condition is often misconstrued. Contrary to popular belief, God does *not* handpick individuals one by one through personal relationships. God will make eternal life available to all of us if and only if we collectively succeed in completing humanity's mission.

Collective failure will not yield individual rewards.

## Mission (n.)

**Definition**: *a specific task with which a person or a group is charged*

While saying each of us has a purpose is technically correct, the more accurate statement is that God created the whole of humanity with a specific mission to complete. Just as the cells in your body can serve individual purposes to help you achieve a personal mission, all the persons in the world can serve unique purposes to help us achieve humanity's mission.

And that mission is to prove that we can overcome our fears and vices to *sustainably* manage our sacred domain: Earth. If Earth becomes uninhabitable, game over. If we kill each other en masse, game over. But if we eliminate the possibility of those game over scenarios, we win.

Who wants us to prove the winning outcome? God. And to whom does God want us to prove this outcome? Satan.

## Satan (n.)

**Definition**: *the rebellious angel who in Christian belief is the adversary of God and lord of evil*

Satan is the most misunderstood character in the Bible. Typically used interchangeably with words like devil and deceiver, Satan has historically been pitted against God in the epic imagery of evil vs. good.

Satan’s actual role in relation to God is best illustrated in the verses of Job 1:6-12. Far from being a reviled enemy, Satan respectfully challenges God much like a trusted advisor. Furthermore, Satan acts with God's permission, meaning that all the evil things people ascribe to Satan are actually inseparable from God. Satan isn't evil. Satan simply expects us to fail where God hopes we will succeed.

Satan and God are partners in the human experiment. Satan challenges God's optimism but does not challenge God's supremacy. And if God is supreme, the only way for the experiment to fail is for humanity to willfully walk a path to destruction instead of salvation.

## Agency (n.)

**Definition**: *the capacity, condition, or state of acting or of exerting power*

Parents derive no joy from raising blindly subservient children. On the flip side, parents experience great pleasure from seeing children grow into self-sufficient adults who are able to make good choices on their own. In this same manner God raised us as a species and is rooting for us.

While God certainly can coerce an ending upon us, forced obedience would prove Satan right and make for a hollow victory. Having already promised us eternal life, God intentionally leaves the rest of the story in our hands.

Will you choose faith that there is no God and let your biological clock run its course? Or will you choose faith in God's promise of eternal life, and commit to finishing the mission? The choice is not God's. It is yours.
