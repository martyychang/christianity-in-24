# Core Tenets

These are the foundational beliefs that shape every chapter of this book. When a drafting decision is ambiguous, these tenets break the tie.

## 1. God is the creator, not the puppeteer

God created the universe, established its laws, and gave humanity free will. He is not micromanaging outcomes. The point of creation is to see what we do with it — and that test only means something if the outcome is genuinely ours.

## 2. God's direct interventions had a defined arc

The Old Testament records God's covenants, prophets, and Law — a progression building toward Jesus. Jesus was the definitive intervention: God entering creation in person to show us what faithfulness looks like. The Bible is the record of that arc. It has a beginning and an end.

## 3. Since the Bible, God is hands-off

Humanity has everything it needs: the example of Jesus, the record of Scripture, and the capacity to reason. God is not "working through" people today in the interventionist sense. Attributing current events, personal decisions, or political outcomes to God's hand undermines human agency and dilutes accountability. When someone does something good, that's a human choosing well — which is the whole point.

## 4. Faith is a choice, not a force acting on you

You choose to believe. You choose to act. You choose to give. That choice has meaning precisely because God isn't pushing you toward it. The Holy Spirit, as described in Scripture, equipped the early church for a specific mission in a specific era. Our book does not claim that the Spirit is actively directing individuals today — because if it were, the test of free will would be meaningless.

## 5. The Bible is a library, not a rulebook

Sixty-six books, roughly forty authors, approximately 1,500 years. It contains history, poetry, law, prophecy, letters, and apocalyptic vision. Reading it requires critical thinking, historical context, and intellectual humility. God gave you a mind and expects you to use it.

## 6. The mission is humanity's to execute

From Chapter 1: humanity's purpose is to prove we can overcome our fears and vices to sustainably manage the earth. Every act of love, justice, and generosity is evidence for the defense. Every act of cruelty, greed, and indifference is evidence for the prosecution. God is watching, not intervening. The verdict depends on us.
