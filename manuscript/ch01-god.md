# Hour 1: God

Q: Why did God create humanity?
A: Because love that exists only for itself isn't love at all.

## Commentary

Let's start with the question everyone asks and almost no one answers simply: who is God?

God is the creator of everything. Not just the earth, not just the universe, but the laws that govern them, the time in which they unfold, and the consciousness with which you're reading this sentence. God existed before any of it, exists outside of all of it, and will exist after all of it is gone.

> In the beginning, God created the heavens and the earth.
— [Genesis 1:1 ESV][1]

That's the opening line of the Bible. No argument, no proof, no apology. Just a declaration. If you're looking for a God who eases you in gently, you're reading the wrong book.

## What does God want?

Here's where people get confused. They assume God created humanity the way a child builds a model train set — for entertainment, or because He could. Some imagine God as a cosmic watchmaker who wound up the universe and walked away. Others think He's keeping score, waiting to punish the losers.

None of that is right.

God created humanity for relationship. Not because He _needed_ one — God is complete in Himself — but because love, by its nature, must have someone to love.

> So God created man in his own image, in the image of God he created him; male and female he created them.
— [Genesis 1:27 ESV][2]

You were made in the _image_ of God. Not as a pet. Not as a servant. As a reflection of God Himself, with the capacity to think, to choose, to love, and to create. That's not a small thing. That's the most dignifying statement ever made about human beings, and it's on the first page.

## Is God sovereign?

Yes. Completely and without exception.

> The LORD has established his throne in the heavens, and his kingdom rules over all.
— [Psalm 103:19 ESV][3]

God is not in a power struggle with Satan. God is not anxious about the state of the world. God is not personally in need of anything you or I can provide. He owns everything ([Psalm 24:1][4]), controls everything ([Daniel 4:35][5]), and knows everything ([Isaiah 46:10][6]).

This raises an obvious question: if God is all-powerful, why does the world look the way it does? Why suffering? Why evil? Why does God allow Satan to operate at all?

We'll get deeper into suffering in a later chapter, but the short answer is this: God's sovereignty doesn't mean God controls every action. It means nothing happens outside of what God _allows_. And what God allows, He allows for a reason — even when that reason is difficult for us to accept.

## Do humans have free will?

Yes. And this is the single most important design decision God made.

God could have created beings who had no choice but to love Him. Robots. Worshipers on autopilot. Instead, He created beings who can look at everything He's done, everything He's offered, and say, "No thanks."

> I call heaven and earth to witness against you today, that I have set before you life and death, blessing and curse. Therefore choose life, that you and your offspring may live.
— [Deuteronomy 30:19 ESV][7]

"Choose." That word only makes sense if the alternative is real. You can choose life, or you can choose death. You can choose God, or you can choose yourself. The choice is genuine, and it's yours.

This is also why the world is broken. Free will means people are free to be selfish, cruel, and destructive. God didn't create evil — He created the _possibility_ of evil by creating the possibility of choice. And He considered that trade-off worth it, because love that is forced isn't love.

## So what does God actually want from you?

Jesus answered this directly. No ambiguity, no fine print.

> You shall love the Lord your God with all your heart and with all your soul and with all your mind. This is the great and first commandment. And a second is like it: You shall love your neighbor as yourself. On these two commandments depend all the Law and the Prophets.
— [Matthew 22:37-40 ESV][8]

That's it. Love God completely. Love other people the way you love yourself. Everything else in the Bible — every law, every prophecy, every parable — hangs on those two commands.

Simple to understand. Extraordinarily difficult to live. And that tension between understanding and living is what the rest of this book is about.

## Questions to sit with

* If God made you in His image, what does that say about how you should treat other people who are also made in His image?
* Do you actually believe you have free will, or do you live as though your circumstances control you?
* When you hear "love God with all your heart," what's the first thing you think of that you're not willing to give up?

That last question is worth being honest about. Whatever came to mind — that's where your journey starts.
